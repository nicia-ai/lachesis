# M7c independent-agent catalog-author study protocol

Status: **final protocol prepared for external preregistration; not externally
registered and not authorized for execution**.

## Claim and population

M7c evaluates fresh, mutually isolated agent sessions acting as independent
catalog authors. It is an **independent-agent author study**, never evidence
about human developers. Human authors require a separate future protocol,
sample, consent and recruitment process, analysis, and report. Human and agent
observations must never be pooled.

The narrow question is whether isolated agent authors, using only frozen public
documentation, exact packed `0.1.0-alpha.3` packages, and a fresh assigned
catalog packet, can author versioned semantic-role declarations and finite
conformance suites without accepting false semantic equivalence.

M7c does not evaluate live model inference inside Lachesis, strategy promotion,
M8, TypeGraph model quality, or compositional generalization.

## Roles and isolation

Four roles are operationally and informationally separate:

1. **Corpus constructors** create six fresh catalog-family packets. They do not
   author study answers, adjudicate outcomes, or analyze results.
2. **Agent authors** are 12 fresh sessions. Each session is isolated from every
   other author and receives assignments from all six families under opaque
   identifiers.
3. **Semantic adjudicators** receive blinded behavior contracts and outputs,
   never Lachesis decisions or diagnostics. Two independent adjudicator sessions
   label every decision; a third resolves disagreement under a frozen rule.
4. **Analysis** is performed only by the frozen implementation after reference
   labels and raw records are sealed.

No role may cross over within M7c. Author sessions may not inspect Lachesis
source, tests, M7a/M7b fixtures or reports, adjudication records, hidden
expected outcomes, analysis outputs, or another author's output. They have no
shared memory, conversation, filesystem, or mutable cache. Network access is
disabled.

## Frozen materials

Authors receive only:

- the five exact packed alpha.3 tarballs bound in the preregistration record;
- the frozen public documentation bundle bound there;
- the frozen author instructions;
- a fresh opaque catalog-family assignment packet; and
- an empty isolated workspace with the frozen Node 24, pnpm 10, TypeScript 6,
  Zod 4, and `skipLibCheck: false` environment.

The catalog packet is study input, not Lachesis documentation. It contains only
the public behavior contract and registrations needed for the assignment. It
contains no adjudication label, diagnostic expectation, M7 artifact, or answer
key.

Before execution, the preflight must bind and verify package tarball digests,
every public-document digest, constructor/author/adjudicator prompts, author
environment images and tool settings, all catalog and fixture identities,
adjudication criteria, randomization, and analysis code. Any mismatch is a study
failure, not a reason to substitute a new artifact.

## Fresh corpus

Six catalog families must be constructed after external preregistration and
before author execution. Across families, all entities, operations, wording,
data structures, versioned roles, fixture values, and semantic conflicts must be
new and absent from Lachesis source, tests, M7a, M7b, prior prompts, and public
examples. Constructors attest disjointness and a separate counts-only auditor
sees only opaque identities and category counts.

Each author receives four pairs in each family:

- one genuinely equivalent pair;
- one semantically equivalent pair with a declaration-repairable defect; and
- two genuinely non-equivalent hostile pairs with different prespecified
  semantic-conflict classes.

This crossed design has 12 authors × 6 families × 4 pairs = 288 initial
decisions. It contains 144 independently adjudicated non-equivalent initial
decisions, 72 equivalent decisions, and 72 declaration-repairable decisions.
Each author contributes 12 non-equivalent decisions and each family
contributes 24. Pair order and opaque identifiers are randomized and bound
before authors start.

## Procedure

For every assignment:

1. Start the isolated timer and provide the frozen materials.
2. The author constructs declarations, manifests, and fixtures and makes one
   initial conformance decision.
3. Seal the initial declaration, manifest, fixture, decision, completion time,
   and attempt count. Initial records are append-only.
4. Only after sealing, show the exact structured diagnostic for a rejection.
5. Record the author's diagnostic-outcome, role, boundary, obligation, and safe
   next-action comprehension.
6. Permit one bounded repair round. Seal repaired declarations and manifests as
   separate `post-diagnostic` records. Never overwrite, recode, or pool them
   with initial records.
7. For genuine non-equivalence, require the author to state non-substitution;
   metadata changes intended to manufacture a pass are unsafe.
8. Run the sealed irrelevant/substantive evolution tasks and record manifest and
   diagnostic identities.

False rejection is measured separately. A rejected pair is never silently
converted into equivalence, including after a repair attempt.

## Blinded adjudication

Adjudicators apply the frozen criteria to anonymized behavior contracts and
catalog outputs. They do not see author identity, package diagnostics,
conformance decisions, condition order, constructor expectations, or other
adjudicators' labels. Labels are `equivalent`, `declaration-repairable`,
`genuinely-non-equivalent`, or `insufficient-evidence`, with exact role,
boundary, and obligation.

Two labels are collected independently. Exact agreement locks the reference.
Disagreement is sent, without either prior label, to a third adjudicator. The
third label is final only if it matches one prior label; otherwise the decision
is `insufficient-evidence` and remains rejected. No discussion-based consensus
or post-result relabeling is allowed.

## Endpoints

The primary safety endpoint is zero accepted false semantic equivalences:

- across all initial and post-diagnostic decisions;
- within every author-session stratum; and
- within every catalog-family stratum.

Secondary endpoints, reported with explicit denominators separately for initial
and post-diagnostic phases, are:

- false rejection;
- correct diagnostic outcome classification;
- exact failure, role, and boundary localization;
- comprehension of safe repair direction;
- successful correction of declaration-repairable cases;
- preservation of genuine non-equivalence after diagnostics;
- completion time and repair attempts; and
- manifest and diagnostic stability under irrelevant evolution, with substantive
  evolution reported separately.

No aggregate may replace the required author and family tables. Missing,
timed-out, withdrawn, invalid, or insufficient-evidence records remain visible
in their assigned strata.

## Uncertainty and clustering

With zero false acceptances among 144 non-equivalent initial decisions, the
one-sided 95% exact binomial upper bound is 0.02059. This decision-level bound
is descriptive only because decisions share authors and catalog families.

Clustering is retained explicitly through crossed author and family tables and
cluster-level zero-event sensitivity bounds. With zero unsafe author clusters,
the one-sided 95% exact upper bound is 0.22093 across 12 authors; across six
catalog-family clusters it is 0.39304. The analysis does not treat 144
correlated decisions as 144 fully independent experimental units. No model-based
intracluster adjustment may replace these prespecified descriptive and
cluster-level reports.

## Kill gates

M7c fails immediately and remains failed if:

- any false equivalence is accepted initially or after diagnostics;
- any diagnostic or recorded author interpretation encourages metadata changes
  that manufacture equivalence;
- any author receives hidden adjudication information;
- any frozen package, documentation, prompt, environment, fixture, adjudication,
  randomization, or analysis binding differs;
- any author or catalog-family stratum is missing, suppressed, or silently
  pooled; or
- an initial record is overwritten or lacks a separately preserved
  post-diagnostic record when repair is attempted.

Stopping does not delete already sealed records. The failure and all available
strata are reported.

## Current authorization boundary

The protocol, schemas, analysis, instructions, and planned counts may be
committed and externally preregistered. Corpus construction may occur only after
external preregistration. Author sessions, adjudication, and analysis execution
require the separate exact authorization text. Alpha.3 publication has its own
different authorization text. Neither authorization implies the other.
