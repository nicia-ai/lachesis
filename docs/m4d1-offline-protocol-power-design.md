# M4d.1 offline protocol and power design

Status: `complete-design-no-go`. Confirmatory corpus generation is blocked as
impractical under the conservative prospective power convention. The candidate
policy remains research-only, non-default, and unconfirmed. No campaign,
manifest, preregistration, executable state, provider call, or final corpus was
created. M4 is closed with mixed conclusions in the [M4 results](m4-results.md).

## Claim boundary and policies

M4d.1 narrows the proposed confirmation to two Anthropic-only paired hypotheses:

1. graph-selected facts versus lexical facts on contradiction tasks; and
2. typed graph versus lexical facts on retraction tasks.

The lexical policy remains the production default and experimental control.
`M4A_INITIAL_POLICY` remains unchanged and development-rejected. The explicitly
named M4d.1 candidate is not a production default:

- Anthropic contradiction → graph facts;
- Anthropic retraction → typed graph; and
- every other provider/category → lexical facts.

The candidate policy digest is
`29121609dde1241c4cfd5fae5053e5fbf3482c3de963b97e0e6fa220e8f3daa7`. The combined
adaptive-policy result would be secondary; neither primary hypothesis implies a
general graph, TypeGraph, provider-independent, or all-task advantage.

## Prospective paired power design

The power calculation uses only frozen M3 development discordances. It does not
reuse M3 as confirmatory evidence.

| Hypothesis                            | Rep 1 favorable-adverse | Rep 2 favorable-adverse | Conservative discordance rate | Shrunk favorable share |
| ------------------------------------- | ----------------------: | ----------------------: | ----------------------------: | ---------------------: |
| Contradiction: graph facts vs lexical |               1-0 of 20 |               1-0 of 20 |                            5% |                    2/3 |
| Retraction: typed graph vs lexical    |               3-0 of 20 |               5-0 of 20 |                           15% |                    4/5 |

The discordance rate is the lower repetition-specific observed rate. Direction
is conservatively add-one shrunk as `(favorable + 1) / (discordant + 2)` using
the lower-discordance repetition. This convention is explicit development
planning, not a confidence bound or confirmatory estimate.

For each hypothesis and each repetition, exact power integrates:

1. the exact binomial distribution of the discordant-pair count; and
2. the exact conditional binomial distribution of favorable directions.

Passage requires the correct direction, at least 20 discordant pairs, and a
two-sided exact McNemar p-value at or below 0.025. The 0.025 design threshold is
the worst-case threshold under a frozen Holm correction across the two primary
hypotheses at family alpha 0.05. Providers and repetitions are never pooled, and
the same conclusion must pass independently in both repetitions.

| Hypothesis    | Cases per repetition |    Exact power | Power at one fewer case |
| ------------- | -------------------: | -------------: | ----------------------: |
| Contradiction |                2,289 | 0.900032071828 |          0.899888564971 |
| Retraction    |                  228 | 0.901532062852 |          0.899932196100 |

This requires 2,517 structurally fresh cases and 10,068 initial calls:
`2,517 cases × 2 views × 2 repetitions`. That exceeds the explicit 500-case
practical design ceiling. M4d.1 therefore stops before corpus generation. The
power-design digest is
`29c80e1348933b232d057e8030d94c0abee53c91f76e79ff5f131a8f36b2366a`.

The complete identity and design record can be reproduced without credentials or
external writes after building the workspace:

```bash
mise exec node@24 -- node apps/benchmark/dist/m4d1-design-cli.js
```

Any future redesign must be prospective. It may choose a different estimand,
power target, minimum-discordance rule, or staged screening design, but it may
not describe a smaller corpus as satisfying this frozen calculation.

## Reduced oracle protocol

The model-visible output is exactly:

```json
{
  "outcome": "answered | insufficient-evidence",
  "answerValues": ["..."],
  "supportingFactIds": ["..."]
}
```

The request contains only the public instruction, public executable answer
contract, exact model-visible evidence, and—when applicable—the previous raw
output plus wire diagnostics or previous typed output plus public semantic
diagnostics. It contains no representation, arm, policy, TypeGraph, storage,
source, expected-answer, or hidden-ground-truth identity.

The runtime performs four separately recorded stages:

1. provider/JSON response decoding;
2. provider-portable wire validation;
3. public semantic validation against the exact visible evidence; and
4. deterministic citation, canonical-path, and provenance reconstruction.

First-attempt end-to-end, first-attempt semantic, post-wire-repair,
post-semantic-repair, and final reliability remain distinct. At most one wire
repair and one semantic repair are permitted. Repair cannot rescue the primary
first-attempt comparison.

## Duplicate-call rule and TypeGraph

Conditions are shared by provider, repetition, and the digest of the exact
model-visible request. If candidate and lexical policies compile to the same
request, the controller must dispatch it once and map that durable result to
both policy outcomes. Provider/repetition boundaries are never deduplicated. The
two focused primary contrasts select different views, so their confirmatory
calls remain genuinely paired rather than duplicated aliases.

TypeGraph 0.38 through `m4c-typegraph-evidence-adapter/1` is the proposed
knowledge-storage substrate, not an experimental arm. Real SQLite integration
tests compare every frozen M3 development case and both public provider
profiles. TypeGraph and in-memory storage produce byte-identical public policy
inputs, selected views, model-visible contexts, request serialization, and
visible-view digests. A future experiment must make one call per unique visible
condition, never one call per storage adapter.

## Unmaterialized protocol probe

The proposed small Anthropic probe uses two development-only fixture slots per
category: one sufficient and one insufficient-evidence condition, each compiled
to its lexical and candidate view.

| Cohort quota                | Maximum |
| --------------------------- | ------: |
| Initial calls               |       8 |
| Wire repairs                |       2 |
| Semantic repairs            |       2 |
| Symmetric transport retries |       4 |
| Provider attempts           |      16 |
| Input tokens                | 128,000 |
| Output tokens               |  32,000 |
| Total tokens                | 160,000 |

Frozen Anthropic introductory pricing charges at most 40,000 µUSD per attempt:
8,000 input tokens at the conservative 2,500,000 µUSD/million cache-write rate,
plus 2,000 output tokens at 10,000,000 µUSD/million. Complete-phase capacity is
therefore 640,000 µUSD. These numbers describe an offline design only; no live
experiment identity, campaign, manifest, state, or spending authority exists.

The unmaterialized probe-design digest is
`dc730952639e833040ccf87418ac9feea3155893c363b777fac8c2cea5d5d8d5`.

Prospective probe kill gates would be:

- all eight initial conditions durable and precisely staged;
- complete answered/insufficient, category, and view coverage;
- zero opaque contract mismatches or SDK/runtime-schema disagreements;
- first-attempt, post-wire, post-semantic, and final outcomes reported
  separately;
- zero hidden-identity leakage, unauthorized calls, or safety violations;
- quota exhaustion or incomplete complete-phase funding is a formal no-go; and
- no confirmatory progression while the corpus-size blocker remains.

## Nonclaims

M4d.1 does not establish an adaptive-policy advantage, graph advantage,
TypeGraph accuracy effect, provider-independent effect, all-task advantage,
production generality, or confirmatory result. It does not externally
preregister or freeze a live experiment. The exact power result is a design
blocker derived from sparse development discordances, not evidence that either
focused hypothesis is true or false.
